Roommate Guide

Feeling nervous about living with someone new is completely normal. Most freshmen share the same worries. Communication and patience go a long way.

How do I set boundaries?

Start with an honest conversation about sleep schedules, guests, noise, and shared items. Setting expectations early helps avoid confusion later.

What if we have completely different personalities?

You do not have to be best friends to be good roommates. Respect, communication, and compromise matter more than having identical interests.

What if my roommate is messy?

Talk about cleanliness early and create a simple cleaning plan. Clear communication prevents frustration from building up.

How do we share space fairly?

Divide closet space, drawers, and common areas evenly. Agree on which items are shared and which are personal.

What if we argue?

Stay calm and address the issue directly. If needed, most campuses offer resident assistants who can help mediate conversations.

Is it normal to feel homesick at first?

Yes. Almost everyone feels overwhelmed during the first few weeks. Give yourself time to adjust and reach out to friends, family, or campus support.

How do I balance privacy and social life?

Schedule quiet time for yourself and communicate when you need space. It is healthy to recharge while also making connections.
